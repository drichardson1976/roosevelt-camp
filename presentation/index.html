Who We Are

We are not
tech people

I'm a basketball coach who runs a summer day camp. My daughter Audrey is 16 and in high school.

Neither of us has ever written a line of code. We don't know what HTML is. We don't know what a database is. We couldn't tell you the difference between a server and a hard drive.

None of that mattered.

The Crazy Idea

"What if we just...

We thought: what's the worst that could happen?

Keeping It Real

Things broke.
A lot.

x

The site got too big

Everything was in one giant file. When we tried to split it up, five things broke at once. We didn't know why - but Claude figured it out.

x

Typing made the cursor disappear

Every form was unusable for a day. We had no idea what was wrong - we just said "it's broken" and sent a screenshot. Claude found and fixed it.

x

Parent accounts vanished

Something went wrong with our data. Parents couldn't log in for days. We sent Claude screenshots of the error and it traced the problem and fixed it.

We never had to understand why things broke. We just had to describe the problem - or screenshot it - and Claude would fix it.

The Secret Sauce

We wrote a cheat sheet
for the AI

Think of it like training a new hire. We wrote a document (Claude actually helped us write it) that tells the AI:

  • - How we like things organized
  • - What mistakes it made before (so it doesn't repeat them)
  • - Always push updates so Audrey and I both have the latest version
  • - An 18-item checklist it follows every single time

The AI reads this before every work session. It never forgets. It never skips steps. It's the most reliable assistant we've ever had.
